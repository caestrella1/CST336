Conversion

Now that we know what the binary system is, how do we convert a binary number back to a decimal number? Let's look at the binary number 1101.

Going from the Least Significant Bit (on the right-most end) to the Most Significant Bit (on the left-most end), we look at each position and get its weight, which here means the base value (2) multiplied by the significance of the digit's position. To better express this, let's look at the binary number 1101 mentioned earlier.

The right-most digit, 1, has a weight of 0. Calculating the value of 20 gives us 1. The digit to its left is a 0 and has a weight of 1. Zeroes, in this case are not used to compute the decimal number and are ignored. We repeat the process as such:

20 + 22 + 23 = 13

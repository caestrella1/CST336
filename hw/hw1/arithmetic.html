Arithmetic

Just like decimal numbers, binary numbers can also be added and subtracted. For simplicity, we will only cover how to add them.

To the right, we are going to be adding the binary numbers 10110 (22) and 1101 (13). Just like base-10 addition, we will start from the right side of the number and find the sum at each position.

To our right, we start by finding the sum of 0 and 1, which is simply 1. Moving one place to the left, we have 1 and 0, which is also 1.

Next, we have 1 and 1, which is 2 (or 10 in binary). Because binary is represented only with 0's and 1's, we cannot simply write a 2 here, the same way we don't write a 10 when adding decimals. Instead, we carry a 1 over to the next column and continue, where we get the same scenario again, and again in the next.

Our final answer is now 100011 (35).
